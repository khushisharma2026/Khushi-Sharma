### **Reflection**

The third unit involved creating a failure wiki in groups. This unit helped me learn a differernt perspective on failure and its role in the learning process. Initially, I used to associate failure with negative outcomes, but this unit introduced me the concept of intelligent failure and how to make failure a roadmap to success.
Our group was assigned the Ariane 5 case study, which highlighted how minor mistakes can lead to significant failures. It also helped me understand that multiple small issues results in a failure. 

While working on wiki, I also realized how crucial teamwork is in achieving good results. Although our group divided the tasks, there was lack of collective understanding of the topic. This became evident during our presentation, where our performance was affected due to lack of coordination.
Initially, I felt disappointed about how bad the presentation went.  However, after reflecting on this experience, I recognized it as an example of intelligence failure. This experience helped me identify the places where I need to improve such as, better communication, teamwork and preparation.
Listening to other group's presentations and the questions asked by the audience also helped me gain more knowledge. I learned about difference between shame and guilt and how they affect our response to failure. I also became aware of other failure case studies. The questions helped me think of real life examples that could be compared with the topics being presented. 

Overall, this unit taught me that failure is not something to be avoided but something to be understood and to be learned from. By reflecting on our mistakes, we can improve and perform better. In the future, I will try to apply these lessons to imrpove both my individual and group performance.
