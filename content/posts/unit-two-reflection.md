### **Reflection**

In this unit, we explored how images can be understood and processed using numbers and mathematical ideas. One of the first topics was learning about pixels and how images are represented digitally. Before this unit, I never really thought about how images are stored or processed inside a computer. It was interesting to learn that images are actually made up of many pixels, and each pixels store numerical values that represent color or brightness.

We also discussed image transformations and how mathematical functions can change an image. While the idea was interesting, I found some of the problems involving functions and graph plotting quite challenging. Understanding how a function transforms pixels values required careful thinking, and it took me some time to grasp the concept.

In addition to image transformations, we also worked on optimization problems. I enjoyed these problems because they required logical thinking and real-life situations. One example I perticularly liked was the train timetable problem. It showed how schedules can be organized to prevent trains from colliding, which made the concept of optimization easier to understand. 

Overall, this unit was both fun and challenging. It helped me understand that images are not only visual but also mathematical in nature. Eventhough some parts were challenging, the activities made the learning process engaging.
