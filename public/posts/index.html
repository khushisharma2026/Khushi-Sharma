Ariane 5 and its failure

At 1985 Ministral conference, ESA (European Space Agency) decided to develop a ‘Future European Launcher’ to replace Ariane 4 and to participate in the space station being proposed for international collaboration by NASA. The Ariane 5 programme was approved at the 1987 Ministral conference. The mission plans included for 1995 Ariane 5 were: Low-Earth orbit, Geostationary and Sun-synchronous. Due to malfunction in the control software, the maiden flight of Ariane 5 (Flight 501) failed 37 seconds after the launch on June 4, 1996. The second test flight (502) on 30th October 1997 was a partial failure. The third test flight (503) on 21st October 1998 was a success. What had caused the failure in launch? Ariane 5 software developers reused code from the Ariane 4 which had been developed 10 years before. The code that caused the problems was in the Inertial Reference System. This was responsible for aligning the rockets before take-off. The developers had added a delay to account for the common inaccuracy of flight launch time, meaning the code was running when it shouldn’t have been. The Inertial Reference System was used to determine whether it was increasing or decreasing, and this is known as horizontal bias or BH value. This value was represented by a 64-bit floating variable. A 64-bit floating point can represent numbers from 10^ -383 to 10^ 384. A 16-bit signed integer can store numbers from -32,768 to 32,767 Problems began to occur when the software attempted to stuff this 64-bit variable into a 16-bit integer. For the first few seconds of flight, the rockets’s acceleration was low, so the conversion between these two values were succesful. However, as the rocket’s velocity increased, the 64-bit variable exceeded 65k, and became too-large to fit in a 16-bit variable. At this point, the processor encountered an opperand error and increased the BH variable with a diagnostic value. The backup Initial Reference System also failed due to the same error condition. At T+37, the BH variable had a diagnostic value from the processor, intended for debugging purposes only.

Ariane 5 Flight 501
Date June 4, 1996
Agency ESA
Outcome Failure (37 seconds after launch)
Cause Software error (overflow)
Loss ~$370 million

The Ariane 5 was developed by the European Space Agency as part of a long-term effort to maintain Europe’s access to space. In the 1985 Ministeral Conference, ESA decided to create a more powerful launch vehicle than Ariane 4 and to supporrt future missions, including taking part in space stations proposed by NASA.
